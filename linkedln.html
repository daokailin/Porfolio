LINKEDLN IOS REDESIGN

DISCOVER VARIOUS PROFESSIONAL MEETING OPPORTUNITIES

Personal Introduction

It's hard to describe my main role in this project. Each of us spent a great amount of effort on intiating the redesign idea and contributed to different parts of this project. From my perspective, my major responsibility was to finalize our design idea and give suggestion to the prototypes and explaination. When we were designing the high-fidelity prototypes, I was mainly focus on how to incorporate other social media app's advantages into our project. Additionally, I was concentrate on the readability and overall layout of the app. Although the aesthetic was the last thing we concerned about, I tried to make sure everything looks nice and clean to attract users' attention.

Project Introduction

The objective of this project is to redesign linkedln app's group feature that will increase users' awareness of off-line meetings, professional networking sessions, and resources while ensuring this piece of information is easily accessible and user friendly. Linkedln users should have more opportunities to meet with local professionals and engage in networking.

Identify the Problems

The current Linkedln mobile app is lacking the basic meetup funtion as a social media platform geared to professionals. The existing "My Network" function doesn't provide users with appropriate meeting opportunities to communicate with people who share the same interests and passions in person.

Thus, we are dedicated to implement a new function that helps users to learn knowledgeable skills and industry experience through "real connection."

User Research Methods

1. We sent out online google form surveys to our friends and colleagues who have used linkedln app before. The goal of using this method was to see how college grduates interact with the existing group feature and find out the trends of their expectation or needs of networking experience.

2. Our team conducted two observational interviews collect more in-depth information about these participants' user experience. We asked them to share their experience of meeting with professionals on linkedln, and we recorded how they used the My Network feature to find their interested groups.

Jinghan is interviewing an experienced linkedln user.

Competitor Analysis

Since group feature has been widely used in other popular social media platforms, we used Competitor analysis to research how well are they served the users and what's their primary features.

We debated between using event based flow to better emphasize the events but opted to use group based flow to better follow the existing design patterns. Discover Groups

Group List

We created several variants for the group list screen. With these alternatives, we tried to explore different ways of presenting event details.

70% of the users stated that they liked the calendar bview since it's a direct visual representation of the event timeline. We simplified the calendar from monthly to weekly in order to retain only necessary informtion and conserve space. Different button colors indicate the event status, and the small icon right next to the event title allows users to import these linkedln events to their favorite calendar apps.

Reflection

This is the first redesign project I've done, but I still found a lot of interesting things during the designing process. The first challenging part was that we had to find out what resources are available to us to re-create the style and layout of the current linkedln app. We were struggled to search for the fonts and other UI elements. Another surprising thing was that we always found redundant feature or element after we finalized an idea, we had to spent more time on creating a brand new feature.

Final Thoughts and Future Plan

Reflecting on our design process, the most exciting feature we developed was the event function that helps different group members to network in professional setting. The data we collected signified that users would like to have various kinds of events other than professional meetings.

For our future plan, we want to build more features and have deep integration with more of the connection tools. Additionally, we want to enlarge our user groups and promote this product to working professionals instead of only focusing on college graduates.
